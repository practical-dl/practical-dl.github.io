4th Workshop on Practical Deep Learning (Practical-DL 2025):
Toward Robust Compressed Foundation Models in the Real World


Workshop at IJCAI 2025



Overview

Compressed foundation models, particularly compressed large language models (LLMs), are increasingly deployed in real-world applications due to their efficiency advantages. Techniques such as quantization, pruning, and distillation significantly reduce memory consumption and latency, making it possible to run these models on resource-constrained devices, such as edge computing platforms and mobile applications. However, compression can also introduce critical robustness and security vulnerabilities, affecting both model reliability and trustworthiness. Compressed models are often more susceptible to adversarial attacks, hallucinations, jailbreak exploits, and degradation in out-of-distribution generalization, raising concerns about their safe deployment in high-stakes applications such as healthcare, finance, and content moderation.

This workshop places a particular emphasis on the robustness challenges of widely deployed compressed foundation models. While existing workshops may discuss general robustness issues in foundation models or focus on efficiency-driven compression, our workshop uniquely targets the intersection of model compression and robustness vulnerabilities. This perspective is crucial because compressed models are now widely used in practice, yet their resilience under real-world threats remains poorly understood and insufficiently addressed. For instance, quantized LLMs are more vulnerable to adversarial prompts, while pruned models may suffer from increased susceptibility to factual errors and hallucinations. Addressing these challenges is not just a theoretical exercise but a pressing need for ensuring secure and reliable deployment.



Call for Papers

Compressed large language models (LLMs) are increasingly adopted in practice due to their efficiency advantages. However, these models pose new challenges in robustness and security, raising concerns for their reliable deployment in real-world scenarios. We welcome research contributions related to the following (but not limited to) topics:
  • Robustness of Compressed Foundation Models
  • Strategies for Jointly Enhancing Compression Efficiency and Robustness
  • Compression for Robust Foundation Models
  • Efficient and Robust Architectural Designs
  • Robust and Efficient Inference Strategies
  • Privacy and Ethical Considerations in Compressed Models
  • Real-World Case Studies on Compressed Model Security and Privacy
Submission Format: Submissions papers (.pdf format) must use the IJCAI Article Template and be anonymized and follow IJCAI 2025 author instructions. The workshop considers two types of submissions: (1) Long Paper [7 pages]; (2) Extended Abstract [4 pages], including figures, tables and references.
Important: Our workshop will feature a Best Paper Award, and certificates will be presented during the workshop.
